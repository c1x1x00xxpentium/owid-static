A brief tour of the past, present and future of global poverty

by Joe Hasell
December 18, 2017 - Last updated on December 18, 2021

The extent of poverty today

There is poverty in every country

Official definitions of poverty differ from country to country, but in high-income countries, like those shown in the chart here, the poverty line is around $30 per day.1

This chart shows the share of the population above and below this threshold for a selection of high-income countries.

We see that not even the richest and most equal countries in the world, like Norway, is without poverty.

A focus on the poorest in the world requires extremely low poverty line

But the majority of the world’s population – 85% – live below $30 a day. In all but the richest countries in the world, almost the entire population fall below this threshold.
