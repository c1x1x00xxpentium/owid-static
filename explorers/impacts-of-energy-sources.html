Methodological notes

Land use of wind energy

Figuring out how to define the land use of wind is difficult. Is it the total area of a wind farm – the area of the box you’d draw around its perimeter? Most of the land between turbines is empty, and might be able to be used for something else, like agriculture. Maybe, then, it is only the land that is directly impacted by the farm – the spots where turbines are planted into the ground and its surrounding excavation.

These two methods are called the project site area and the direct impact area. Both are used in different contexts.

In the UNECE report, wind energy is measured only by its direct impact area. While useful, this is rarely the land use measure that most people have in mind when they think about wind energy. Instead, we have used the project site area – the perimeter of a wind farm. To calculate this we have used two prominent wind farms (which are shown in the chart below for the minimum and maximum: for the ‘minimum’ point, we have used Whitelees wind farm in the UK. For the ‘maximum’ point we have used Monte-des-Quatre Faux in France. The ‘median’ is the average of these two extremes. Whitelees wind farm in the UK uses 46 m² per MWh. In contrast, France’s biggest onshore farm – Monte-des-Quatre-Faux – uses 101 m² per MWh.

Variability in impacts within a given energy source

In the Data Explorer, we present the global average values for a given impact for each source. Note, however, this can vary significantly depending on the specific context. An example of this is given for the land use of electricity sources below.

The land use of different energy sources can vary significantly, depending on the context of the production site – its location; climate; and choices around the density and spacing of equipment. For example, the spacing of wind turbines and solar panels will affect how much land is needed per unit of electricity output. In the chart here we show not only the median impact for each source, but also the maximum and minimum in the UNECE meta-analysis for context on how these values vary.
